About Us

Akina Ozele Farm is a registered farm investment enterprise established in 2025. The farm is committed to integrating forestry, livestock, and crop production into a sustainable agricultural ecosystem that promotes environmental conservation, food security, and economic growth.

Vision & Mission

🌍 Vision

To design a biodiverse, self-sustaining agricultural ecosystem that produces high-value crops, ethically raised livestock, and timber while regenerating soil health and capturing carbon.

🎯 Mission

To build a profitable, self-sustaining agricultural model that integrates forestry, livestock, and crop production while minimizing waste and improving soil health.

🏆 Overall Goal

To create a closed-loop ecosystem where each enterprise supports the others, maximizing biodiversity, soil health, and year-round economic resilience.

Strategic Goals (2026 - 2031)

  • Convert livestock manure and crop residues into high-quality organic compost.
  • Establish agroforestry systems, boundary tree planting and silvopasture systems.
  • Implement rainwater harvesting and soil conservation measures.
  • Increase value addition through processing and packaging of farm products.
  • Work towards organic certification for premium market opportunities.
  • Promote active participation and leadership of women in agricultural development.

Target

Akina Ozele Farm aims to develop and strengthen agribusiness ventures to achieve an annual turnover of UGX 1 Billion while empowering over 5,000 neighboring farmers through knowledge sharing, innovation, and sustainable agricultural practices.

SWOT Analysis

💪 Strengths

Availability of skilled farming practitioners, supportive agricultural frameworks, and access to modern technologies.

⚠ Weaknesses

High mechanization costs, expensive technologies, and limited adoption of innovations.

🚀 Opportunities

Growing food demand, regional markets, value addition, agricultural insurance, and expanding urban populations.

🌩 Threats

Climate change, pests and diseases, inflation, price fluctuations, and unpredictable environmental conditions.
